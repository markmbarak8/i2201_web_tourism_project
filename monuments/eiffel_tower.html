The Eiffel Tower

Explore this majestic iron building.

Dominating the Parisian skyline since 1889, the Eiffel Tower—originally built as a temporary entrance for the Exposition Universelle—is now the world’s most recognized symbol of French elegance and industrial triumph. Standing 330 meters tall on the Champ de Mars, this "Iron Lady" was once the tallest structure on Earth, a record it held for over 40 years. Whether you are watching its golden lights sparkle against the night sky or looking down through the thrilling glass floor on the first level, the tower offers an unrivaled perspective of the City of Light. From its intricate lattice of 18,000 iron parts to the panoramic vistas that stretch 70 kilometers across the French capital, a visit to the Eiffel Tower is more than just sightseeing; it is an essential encounter with a masterpiece of global history.

The Eiffel Tower is a marvel of wrought-iron engineering, composed of 18,038 individual pieces joined together by over 2.5 million rivets. Its iconic lattice design was not merely aesthetic; it was a revolutionary solution to wind resistance, allowing the structure to sway slightly in storms without breaking. Every diagonal beam and curve was mathematically calculated by Gustave Eiffel’s team to distribute the massive weight of 10,100 tons while maintaining a surprisingly light and airy profile that transformed the definition of modern architecture.

Beyond its iron skeleton, the interior of the tower blends historical charm with high-tech ingenuity. Visitors ascending the pillars can still see the massive hydraulic pistons and giant flywheels that powered the original 19th-century elevators. Inside the floors, the experience transitions from the industrial—with exposed steel beams and rivets—to the luxurious, featuring floor-to-ceiling glass walls and heated observation decks. Recent renovations have added ecological features like wind turbines and solar panels, ensuring this 130-year-old monument remains a leader in sustainable design.

Getting to the Tower

The Eiffel Tower is easily accessible via the Paris public transport network:

By Metro: The closest station is Bir-Hakeim (Line 6). For the most dramatic first view, get off at Trocadéro (Lines 6 & 9) and walk across the bridge.

By RER: Take Line C to the Champ de Mars-Tour Eiffel station, which is just a short walk from the entrance.

By Bus: Lines 42, 82, and 72 all stop within a few minutes' walk of the tower.
